# Sustainability, Data & Critical Thinking for Diverse Learners

## A Pilot Course for Corporates, & Sustainability Platforms

**Primary SDGs:** 4, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13  
**Focus:** Critical reading, global sustainability trends, research analysis, varied perspectives, action plans, futuristic implementations, impacts, reflection

> **Pilot Status:** This is a **pilot course** eligible for case study documentation and continuous improvement. Issues, suggestions, and pull requests are welcome.

---

## Overview

This modular course is designed for a **diverse group of learners** across age groups and professional backgrounds, including:

- Corporate clients (e.g., **Sustainability focus groups** users)
- Employees from sustainability-focused organizations
- Learners from **sustainability learning platforms**
- Youth and adult learners interested in sustainability and data

The course is hosted on **GitHub** as a living, version-controlled resource that can be tested, reviewed, and improved over time.

---

## Aim

To equip diverse learners with the **knowledge, skills, and tools** to:

- Critically understand global sustainability issues framed by the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)
- Interpret sustainability data and trends
- Design evidence-based, context-specific action plans
- Monitor, evaluate, and reflect on outcomes to improve future actions

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of the course, learners will be able to:

1. Explain core sustainability concepts and how they connect to selected SDGs (4, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13)
2. Critically read and assess articles, reports, ESG disclosures, and infographics on climate, energy, resources, and equity
3. Analyze sustainability datasets (emissions, energy mix, waste, ESG indicators) and derive insights
4. Compare varied perspectives on sustainability issues (government, business, civil society, local communities)
5. Design, document, and present an action plan with clear goals, indicators, and timelines
6. Reflect on outcomes, limitations, and future improvements using simple monitoring and evaluation (M&E) logic

---

## SDG Focus

The course uses an **integrated SDG mix** as its backbone:

- **SDG 4:** Quality Education – learning for sustainable development and critical thinking
- **SDG 7:** Affordable and Clean Energy – renewables, energy efficiency, transitions
- **SDG 10:** Reduced Inequalities – fairness, inclusion, just transition
- **SDG 11:** Sustainable Cities and Communities – urban life, mobility, infrastructure
- **SDG 12:** Responsible Consumption and Production – waste, materials, circularity
- **SDG 13:** Climate Action – mitigation, adaptation, resilience

## Pilot, Case Study & Continuous Improvement

This course is a **pilot** designed to be documented as a **case study** in sustainability education.

The `/pilot_case_study/` folder will contain:

- **Context:** learner profiles, client types (sustainability focus groups, sustainability platforms)
- **Iterations:** changes made to modules based on feedback
- **Feedback:** summaries of learner input (issues, discussions)
- **Outcomes:** what worked, what didn't, and impact evidence
- **Lessons Learned:** recommendations for future versions

Learners and facilitators can:

- Open **GitHub Issues** to suggest improvements or report problems
- Submit **Pull Requests** with new content, examples, or refinements
- Contribute anonymized work (action plans, impact reports) as pilot case studies

## How to Use This Course

### For Learners

1. Start with **Module 1** and proceed sequentially
2. Complete all activities and reflections in each module
3. Use `/resources/` for datasets, templates, and rubrics
4. If accessing paid services, coordinate with the facilitator

### For Facilitators & Organizations

1. Clone or fork this repository
2. Adapt modules for your context (corporate training, community programs, university courses)
3. Use the `/services/` structure to offer paid notes, case studies, and support
4. Document your pilot implementation in `/pilot_case_study/`
