# Module 4: Case Studies & Varied Perspectives for Corporates

**Primary SDGs:** 4, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13

## Overview
Uses real-world case studies to help corporate learners understand sustainability issues from multiple perspectives. Examines how companies, governments, communities, and civil society approach the same issue differently.

## Content Topics
- Case study types: success stories, failures/controversies, in-progress/ambiguous cases
- Case Theme A: Energy transition & corporate decarbonization (SDG 7, 13)
- Case Theme B: Urban operations & sustainable cities (SDG 11, 12)
- Case Theme C: Supply chain sustainability & inequality (SDG 8, 10, 12)
- Case Theme D: Climate risk, adaptation & inequality (SDG 10, 11, 13)
- Stakeholder mapping: internal (board, employees, unions) and external (customers, investors, regulators, communities, NGOs)
- Trade-offs: cost vs sustainability, speed vs inclusion, global vs local
- Unintended consequences: displacement, inequality, lock-in to technologies
- Just transition: ensuring fairness in sustainability transitions (SDG 10)

## Activities
1. **Case Study Analysis (Single Case)** — Select one case (company decarbonization, urban sustainability project, or supply chain initiative). Write 600-800 words: context, core problem, stakeholders, actions taken, outcomes, SDG links
2. **Stakeholder Perspective Mapping** — Map at least 4 stakeholder groups. For each: interests/concerns, power/influence, how affected, how represented in corporate communications. Reflect on which perspectives are visible/missing
3. **Improved Corporate Action Plan** — Design an improved/alternative action plan (500-700 words): goal, specific actions, resources needed, how it addresses stakeholder concerns/trade-offs/SDGs, simple monitoring indicator

## Assessment
- Depth and clarity of case analysis
- Quality of stakeholder perspective map and reflection (completeness, insight, awareness of bias)
- Feasibility and SDG alignment of improved action plan (practicality, stakeholder consideration, trade-off awareness)

## Reflection Prompt
- Which case theme resonated most with your own work and why?
- What surprised you about the perspectives of different stakeholders?
- What trade-off is hardest to navigate in your organization's sustainability efforts?
- How will you apply insights from this case to your own context?
