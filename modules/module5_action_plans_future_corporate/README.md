# Module 5: Action Plans & Futuristic Implementations for Corporates

**Primary SDGs:** 4, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13

## Overview
Guides corporate learners to design concrete, future-oriented action plans that address sustainability challenges. Uses scenario thinking to anticipate future risks and opportunities.

## Content Topics
- Action plan components: goals, actions, timelines, responsibilities, resources, risks, indicators
- Linking to corporate priorities: cost reduction, efficiency, innovation, risk management, investor/customer expectations
- Scenario thinking: exploring different plausible futures (e.g., high regulation vs low regulation)
- Key uncertainties: carbon price level, renewable energy cost, policy stringency, climate impacts
- SDG alignment: mapping actions to specific SDG targets (e.g., SDG 7.2, SDG 13.2)
- Impact dimensions: environmental (emissions, energy, waste), social (jobs, skills, equity), governance (transparency, accountability)
- Indicators: measurable variables to track progress (kWh, tons CO₂, training hours)
- Barriers and enablers: cost, policy, culture, technology
- Adaptive management: adjusting actions based on monitoring results and changing conditions

## Activities
1. **Draft a Corporate Sustainability Action Plan** — Design an action plan for your organization/business unit. Include: goal, SDG links (at least 2), 3-6 specific actions with responsibilities/timeline, resources, risks & mitigation, 2-4 indicators
2. **Scenario Thinking Exercise** — Identify one key uncertainty. Develop 2 contrasting scenarios (e.g., stringent policy vs weak policy). For each: how context changes, how action plan performs, what additional actions needed. Identify robust actions and early warning signals
3. **Impact & Reflection Note** — Write 400-600 words: expected impacts (environmental/social/economic), who benefits/bears costs (SDG 10), potential unintended consequences, how plan might adapt, what you learned about future planning, what support you need

## Assessment
- Clarity, feasibility, and SDG alignment of action plan
- Depth and insight of scenario thinking exercise (realism, consideration of risks, robustness)
- Quality of impact & reflection note (awareness of distributional effects, unintended consequences, adaptive management)

## Reflection Prompt
- What was most challenging about designing a future-oriented action plan?
- How has scenario thinking changed your view of risks and opportunities?
- What is one thing you will do differently in your work as a result of this module?
- What support do you need from your organization to implement your action plan?
