# Module 2: Global Trends & Data for Corporates

**Primary SDGs:** 4, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13

## Overview
Builds on Module 1 by introducing global sustainability trends through data. Learners explore key trends in energy, emissions, cities, consumption, and inequality, and learn to read sustainability data critically.

## Content Topics
- Major global trends: climate & emissions (SDG 13)
- Energy transition: renewables, fossil fuels, access gaps (SDG 7)
- Urbanization & cities: population growth, infrastructure (SDG 11)
- Consumption & resources: material use, waste, circularity (SDG 12)
- Inequality & justice: income gaps, unequal climate impacts (SDG 10)
- Data literacy: reading charts, tables, indicators
- Data sources: UN, IEA, World Bank, corporate disclosures
- Critical interpretation: source credibility, bias, limitations

## Activities
1. **Trend Mapping with Global Data** — Choose two global trends, create/annotate a visualization, write 3-4 bullet points on trend direction and corporate implications
2. **Corporate Claim vs External Data** — Compare a corporate net-zero/renewable claim with global/sectoral emissions trends. Assess ambition and gaps
3. **Data Insight Note for Business Decision** — Write 300-400 words linking a global trend to organizational risks/opportunities and propose one concrete action

## Assessment
- Completeness and clarity of trend mapping
- Quality of claim vs data analysis (critical thinking, use of evidence, identification of limitations)
- Relevance and actionability of data insight note (clear link to business decisions and SDGs)

## Reflection Prompt
- Which global trend surprised you most and why?
- How has your understanding of sustainability data changed?
- What is one data question you now ask more often when reading corporate or policy reports?
- What additional data or tools would help your organization respond better to these trends?
