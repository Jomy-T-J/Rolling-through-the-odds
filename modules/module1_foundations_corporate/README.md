# Module 1: Foundations of Sustainability for Corporates

**Primary SDGs:** 4, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13

## Overview
Introduction to core sustainability concepts, ESG, and SDGs in a corporate context. Builds critical reading skills and connects business strategy to global sustainability challenges.

## Content Topics
- What is sustainability? Definition and business relevance
- ESG fundamentals: Environmental, Social, Governance
- The 17 SDGs overview (focus: 4, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13)
- Materiality and double materiality for corporations
- Scope 1, 2, 3 emissions introduction
- Just transition and inequality (SDG 10)
- Why sustainability matters for business: risk, opportunity, strategy
- Critical reading basics for sustainability texts

## Activities
1. **SDG Mapping for a Business Unit** — Map how your business unit affects each SDG (4, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13) and identify improvement opportunities
2. **Critical Reading of Two Documents** — Read a corporate ESG report excerpt and a news/policy article. Compare claims and evidence
3. **Business Case Statement** — Draft a 250-word business case linking a sustainability initiative to value creation and at least two SDGs

## Assessment
- Completion of SDG mapping table
- Quality of critical reading note (clarity, use of evidence, consideration of perspectives)
- Coherence of business case paragraph (clear link to business value and SDGs)

## Reflection Prompt
- What surprised you about the link between your business unit and the SDGs?
- What is one assumption you had about sustainability that changed?
- What is one question you still have about how corporate actions affect global sustainability issues?
