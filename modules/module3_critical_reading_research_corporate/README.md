# Module 3: Critical Reading & Research Analysis for Corporates

**Primary SDGs:** 4, 7, 10, 11, 12, 13

## Overview
Develops critical reading and research analysis skills in a corporate context. Learners analyze corporate ESG reports, policy documents, news articles, and NGO briefs on sustainability issues.

## Content Topics
- Critical reading: active, questioning reading that analyzes arguments and evidence
- Claim, evidence, reasoning: identifying main claims, supporting data, and logic
- Assumptions: underlying beliefs that must be true for arguments to hold
- Bias and perspective: how position and interest shape what is included/excluded
- Types of texts: corporate ESG reports, policy documents, news articles, NGO/academic reports
- Evaluating evidence: source credibility, data quality, alternative explanations
- Comparing perspectives: corporate vs NGO vs government vs community views
- Materiality: issues most significant to business value and stakeholder impact

## Activities
1. **Critical Reading Worksheet (Single Document)** — Choose one sustainability text (ESG report, policy brief, news/NGO report). Complete worksheet identifying main claims, evidence, assumptions, and biases
2. **Multi-Perspective Comparison** — Compare 2-3 texts on the same issue from different perspectives (company, NGO, government). Summarize claims, compare evidence, identify agreements/disagreements
3. **Research Analysis Note for Corporate Decision** — Write 500-700 words on a sustainability issue relevant to your organization. Include problem/question, evidence summary, perspectives, uncertainties, implications, and SDG links

## Assessment
- Completeness and depth of critical reading worksheet
- Quality of multi-perspective comparison (clarity, balance, use of evidence, identification of bias)
- Rigor and relevance of research analysis note (problem definition, evidence use, consideration of perspectives, SDG linkage)

## Reflection Prompt
- What was hardest about critically reading sustainability texts?
- Which type of source (company, NGO, government, media) do you tend to trust most, and why?
- How has your approach to reading changed after this module?
- What is one critical reading habit you want to keep using in your work?
